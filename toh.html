The Owl House Season 3

About the Show

The Owl House is a Disney Channel animated show about a young girl named Luz who feels like a misfit at home, but stumbles upon another dimension where she meets a witch named Eda the Owl Lady and an adorable demon named King who both also feel like misfits. Since Luz is supposed to be at summer camp anyway, she decides to stay and learn how to be a witch from Eda.

Animation

At face value, the animation in this show is very similar to many other Disney Cartoon shows. However, the animation of the different characters magic use and expecially the magical fight scenes make this show really stand out. The lighting and the choreography of these scenes are incredible, especially those in the show's finale.

Social Implications

The Owl House pushed the boundaries of representation in Disney Cartoons by including several LGBTQIA+ characters, but particularly the main character. Luz identifies as bisexual and dates a girl at her school, Amity. Additionally, Eda's main love interest in the show, Raine, is non-binary. This of course was very meaningful to many fans of the show. LGBTQIA+ fans loved seeing characters who they could relate to in such a wonderfully wholesome and adventurous cartoon. However, the Disney execs did not share their perspective. Without the creator, Dana Terrace's knowledge, the executives met and decided to cancel the show under the guise of the show "not reaching its intended age group." Though it cannot be proven that this was in fact the homophobia of Disney corporate rearing its ugly head, it is awfully convenient that this news of cancellation came shortly after the production of an episode where Luz and Amity became an official couple.
