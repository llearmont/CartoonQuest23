Elemental

About the Movie

Elemental is a movie produced by Pixar animation studio and is the studio's first attempt at a true rom-com since Wall-E. The movie depicts a world where there are different races of people based on the elements of fire, water, earth and air all living in Element City. Water people make up the majority and the city has been very much designed for them and as a result, is not very safe for fire people. The movie follows a fire family who immigrate to Element City in the hopes of building a better life for their daughter, but this proves to be more difficult than they had anticipated. The fire community is very segregated from the rest of Element City and they are at several disadvantages due to how risky being close to water can be. Ember feels she has a very clear picture of how things work here and how elements simply cannot mix until she meets Wade, a water guy.

Animation

Pixar animation nevers fails to impress and Elemental is no exception. The animators put so much effort into the physics and movement of the characters. The fire people are constantly moving and their flames are constantly burning and flickering depending on their surroundings. The water people are constantly dripping and can easily reshape themselves, while the water that their bodies are composed up is constantly rippling. The different elements have the opacities that you would expect encountering them in the real world, which creates some really complex and beautiful lighting scenarios.

Social Implications

Elemental is a beautiful and simple way to depict a interracial relationship and all the struggles that come with it. Ember has been with her family her whole life and has never really had much chance to consider what her life might be like outside of her parents' "bubble." She feels pressured into taking over her father's shop, but she struggles with interacting with customers. When Wade first splashes into her life, he is not a welcome presence. He is a city inspector who feels he is obligated to report all of Ember's father's shop's safety violations. He sends in his report moments before hearing Ember's story and how much this shop means to her family. He immediately feels terrible and the two team up to reverse the tickets and make sure the shop is safe. Through their time together, Ember sees how sweet and emotionally intelligent Wade is and she begins to fall for him. She is conflicted because she knows her parents would not approve of her dating a water guy, but Wade's genuine empathy is irresistible. He's the mature and loving presence she really needs to gain the courage to chase her dreams. It not only speaks to the marginalized and those in interracial relationships but it depicts a beautiful picture of how to grow and mature in one's mental health.
